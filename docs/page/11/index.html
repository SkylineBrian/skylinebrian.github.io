About

Welcome to the Geospatial Network Analysis and Visualization (GeoNAVI) Lab. The lab is led by Dr. Yujie Hu, Associate Professor of Spatial Networks in the Department of Geography at the University of Florida.

We are a research group with interests in studying urban–such as transportation, human mobility, and accessibility–from a geographic perspective. GIScience and Network Science are the disciplinary foundations of our research, but our research also draw on many other fields that study urban, such as transportation engineering, social networks, urban planning, public health, criminology, and environmental science and policy. Join Us

We are always looking for enthusiastic and self-motivated critical thinkers–undergraduate students, graduate students, or visiting scholars–to join the GeoNAVI lab. People in the lab are passionate about urban research and have experience in some combination of spatial analysis, network theory and analysis, statistics, and geovisualization. Relevant disciplines include, but are not limited to, Geography, GIScience, transportation engineering, network science, urban planning, public health, criminology, and environmental science and policy. Any questions can be directed to Dr. Yujie Hu at yujiehu@ufl.edu.

People

Principal Investigator

Dr. Yujie Hu — Associate Professor, Department of Geography, University of Florida

Email: yujiehu (at) ufl.edu
Office: Turlington Hall 3133
Phone: 352-294-7514
Affiliate Faculty: UF Informatics Institute, UF Transportation Institute, Anita Zucker Center for Excellence in Early Childhood Studies

Dr. Hu’s research and teaching focus on urban transportation, human mobility, and accessibility. His current work centers on:

  1. relationships between people’s mobility within cities—including commuting, healthcare-seeking, and crime—and the urban built environment;
  2. spatial accessibility to opportunities such as jobs, healthcare, food, and transportation infrastructure, and how it is affected by natural hazards; and
  3. network flow analysis and optimization of travel patterns related to commuting, bike sharing, healthcare, and food delivery.
